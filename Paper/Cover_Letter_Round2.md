Dear Dr. William Clarke and the Editorial Board,  

We are pleased to resubmit the revised version of our manuscript, now titled "**Early Prediction of Gallstone Disease Using Validated Machine Learning Models on Bioimpedance and Laboratory Data**" (Reference No. CCACTA-D-26-00706), for consideration for publication in *Clinica Chimica Acta*.  

We have carefully reviewed the constructive feedback from Reviewer #5 and Reviewer #6 in this second round of review and have fully revised the manuscript to address all points raised. We believe these revisions have significantly strengthened the methodological transparency and reporting quality of our work.  

Specifically, we have implemented the following major enhancements in this revision:  
1. **Collinearity Analysis**: Conducted a formal diagnostics check by calculating the **Variance Inflation Factor (VIF)** for all features, presenting the results in a new table (Table 3), and describing how deterministic collinearity is handled.  
2. **Feature Ablation Study**: Isolated the contribution of our feature engineering strategy by running a 10-fold cross-validation ablation study on the public dataset, comparing Raw, Stage 1 FE, and Stage 2 FE feature sets (Table 4).  
3. **Comparative Literature Review**: Added a comprehensive comparison table (Table 5) side-by-side with prior studies (Esen et al. 2024, Li et al. 2025, Chakraborty et al. 2025) on the same dataset.  
4. **Tone and Formatting Refinement**: Formatted our core contributions as bullet points in the Introduction, corrected a typographical error in Figure 1, and carefully toned down causal phrases (e.g., "risk drivers") and speculative claims about prospective diagnostic delay reductions, as requested.  
5. **Methodological Justifications**: Added details regarding local cohort missingness, SMOTE class distribution details, and clinical/mathematical justifications for selecting SMOTE, Random Search, and SHAP over alternatives.

All authors have reviewed and approved this resubmission. We confirm that this work is original, has not been published elsewhere, and is not under consideration by another journal.
